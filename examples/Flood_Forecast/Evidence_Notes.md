# Evidence Notes v2 - AI Flood Forecasts

## Source

Primary source: `../sources/AI Increases Global Access to Reliable Flood Forecasts.pdf`.

## Extracted Evidence Used in v2

- Page 1: The paper reports reliability in predicting extreme riverine events in ungauged watersheds up to a 5-day lead time, similar to or better than GloFAS nowcasts.
- Page 1: The paper reports accuracy over 5-year return-period events similar to or better than current accuracies over 1-year return-period events.
- Page 1-2: The model was incorporated into an operational early warning system producing free/open real-time forecasts in over 80 countries through FloodHub.
- Page 3: The AI model uses Long Short-Term Memory networks to predict daily streamflow through a 7-day forecast horizon.
- Page 3: The paper reports out-of-sample testing using random k-fold cross-validation across 5,680 streamflow gauges, plus additional splits by terminal watersheds, climate zones, and continents.
- Page 3: GloFAS version 4 is used as the benchmark.
- Page 3-5: Evaluation reports precision, recall, and F1 over return-period events and lead times.
- Page 5: The paper notes both AI and GloFAS show location-based differences in reliability, with continent-level differences and exceptions in Africa/Asia for some return-period cases.
- Page 7: The paper discusses predicting forecast reliability in ungauged basins, noting local reliability can be hard to evaluate without ground-truth data.
- Methods: The paper describes 1984-2021 gauge data, 5,680 evaluation gauges, cross-validation across time and space, and an open-source NeuralHydrology research implementation.

## Typology Evidence

This is a Contributing Factors / Environmental Hazard Forecasting model. It predicts riverine flood hazard and streamflow, not food security outcomes. IPC relevance is indirect and depends on local food-security transmission pathways.

## Evidence Boundaries

- The sample does not verify the current FloodHub operational model version, service coverage, API/access conditions, or dashboard communication.
- The sample does not evaluate local flood exposure, impact data, or food-security transmission pathways for a specific IPC country/cycle.
- Context-specific judgments are illustrative unless completed by IPC-GSU, country/regional analysts, and IPC-LACI Team for a real operational context.
