# Evidence Notes v2 - Acute Child Malnutrition Forecasting

## Source

Primary source: `../sources/constenla-villoslada-et-al-2025-high-frequency-monitoring-enables-machine-learning-based-forecasting-of-acute-child.pdf`.

## Extracted Evidence Used in v2

- Page 1: The paper argues that high-frequency child anthropometry from sentinel sites can enable reasonably accurate forecasts at time horizons up to 6 months.
- Page 1-2: The work is connected to Kenya's National Drought Management Authority and focuses on Kenya's arid and semi-arid counties where food emergencies occur regularly.
- Page 2: NDMA field enumerators collect monthly household survey data to measure MUAC among a rotating panel of children.
- Page 2: The paper uses 3,616,129 child-and-month MUAC observations from January 2006 to December 2020 to generate ward/month U5 GAM prevalence, yielding 21,643 observations.
- Page 2: GAM is defined per WHO standards as MUAC less than 125 mm.
- Page 2-3: The paper compares secondary-data, time-series-only, and hybrid models and evaluates monthly forecasts at 1, 3, 6, 9, and 12 months.
- Page 3: Out-of-sample performance is evaluated using OOS R2, sensitivity, specificity, and precision.
- Page 3: Alert cases are defined using a WHO-related threshold where wasting prevalence exceeds 15 percent.
- Page 3-6: Performance varies by period, including changes after sentinel-site location changes and periods where GAM temporal autocorrelation differs.
- Page 6: The hybrid model is identified as the preferred specification because it combines time-series information with secondary-data forecasting power.
- Page 8: The paper presents potential operational outcomes from predictive maps.

## Typology Evidence

This is an Outcome Indicator Model because the target is measured GAM prevalence. It is AMN-relevant but is not automatically IPC AMN classification or official IPC AMN evidence.

## Evidence Boundaries

- The sample does not verify code, implementation, current data access, or operational model version.
- The sample does not assess whether the model output satisfies IPC AMN evidence protocols.
- The context-specific judgment is illustrative because it is not tied to a specific IPC AMN analysis cycle or country request.
